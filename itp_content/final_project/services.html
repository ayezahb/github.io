Committed Mentorship

Committed Mentorship (CM) for One Semester or the Entire School Year

Mentors and Mentees will remain in contact for one of the indicated periods of time until the end date stipulated at the start of mentoring. Mentors may or may not be expected to fulfill the other core services listed below, the execution of which are up to the final decision of both parties. All efforts of the mentor should be geared toward preparing the mentee for the best possible outcome in the college application process. Both Mentor and Mentee must consistently and frequently communicate with each other, for the benefit of the mentee and the convenience of the mentor.

Timing of meetings: Flexible

**Both parties may meet every week or once a month if desired, but timing between each meeting should not exceed one month.

Building College Applications

Building College Applications

Under the guidance of their assigned mentors, mentees will learn how to put their applications together on either the CommonApp, Coalition, UC Applications, etc., in order to effectively display themselves in a genuine and appealing manner. Mentees will align critical components of the application (course choices, extracurriculars, volunteer experience, and leadership positions (if applicable)) with their perceived ability to adapt to college as well as their future goals.

Expected time commitment may be adjusted based on preference: Every two weeks or Once a month

Essay Proofreading

Essay Proofreading

Mentors assigned to this particular role will be expected to read through their mentees’ shared essays, offering suggestions to improve the quality of the compositions with regard to elements such as grammar, concision, coherence of ideas, flow of paragraphs, and relevance to mentee’s application.

Expected time commitment will be one of the most flexible out of all service options due to accessible electronic documents ( via Google Docs, Microsoft, e-mail), but possible meetings (for reference) may be held:

  • Every week
  • Every two weeks
  • Once a month (at the latest)
  • Times are adjustable and not restricted to these options.

College Information Sessions & Networking

College Information Sessions & Networking

Mentors may host presentations for the institutions they are currently attending via online sessions. This provides an opportunity for interested mentees to delve deeper into these schools and determine what aspects of each college/university they like or dislike.

Alternatively, students may connect with a group of mentors via online sessions (or in-person if circumstances permit) to find out what makes a strong college applicant.

Sessions may be done in Q&A style for organizational purposes
